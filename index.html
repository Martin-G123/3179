Key Producers and Consumers

The oil market has been diversified by more players over time. Historically, much of the world's production and consumption has been led by a few key market players. Countries with strong economies. As the world has modernised, with many countries developing their own industry and economies, production and consumption has been more evenly split between more countries.

Net Importers vs. Net Exporters

To the right we see a log-log representation of production vs. consumption of oil. Countries below the line are net importers, while countries above the line are net exporters. European countries are generally net importers, while many Asian countries, particularly those in the Middle East, are net exporters.

The Connection between Oil Prices and Production

The Relationship between oil price and production is such that small overall changes in production create big fluctuations in price. This may be explained by the world's relatively inelastic demand for petroleum products, meaning small fluctuations in production can have larger impacts on price.

Further Exploration of Price and Production

Here we can see a more detailed breakdown for the correlation between production and pricing. We can see how this applies to individual countries. Any directional pattern between the light and dark coloured dots on the right indicates a relationship between price and oil production.

Trends in Production and Consumption of Oil, with Price Factors

A deep dive into the demographics and market dynamics shaping global oil production, consumption, and pricing.

How Much Energy is 1 TWh of Oil?

1.6 million barrels approximately — enough to fill 102 Olympic-size swimming pools. This exceeds Australia’s daily consumption of 1.1 million barrels of oil.

Global Variations in Production

Big variations in production can be seen across countries, and even within the same country over time. Factors such as demand, pricing, production capacity, and market strategy drive these differences.

Key Producers and Consumers

Historically, oil markets have been led by a few major players — often nations with strong economies. Over time, more countries have developed industrial capabilities, leading to more balanced global production and consumption.

Net Importers vs. Net Exporters

This log–log chart compares oil production and consumption. Countries below the diagonal line are net importers, while those above it are exporters. European countries tend to be importers, while Middle Eastern nations are typically exporters.

The Connection Between Oil Prices and Production

Oil prices can fluctuate dramatically with small production changes due to the inelastic nature of global demand. Even minor disruptions or increases in production capacity can significantly affect market prices.
